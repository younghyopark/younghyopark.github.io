## General Version of Model-Free PI

### Algorithm

- Assume a nonlienar architecture $$Q(s,a) \approx Q_\theta(s,a)$$. 

- **(Approximate Policy Evalution)** Evaluate the approximate Q-function $Q_\theta ^\pi$ for current policy $\pi$

- (Approximate Policy Improvement) Generate imporved policy $\pi'$

  $$
  \pi'(s) = \arg \max_{a\in A} Q_\theta ^\pi (s,a)
  $$

Again, our problem is the policy evaluation step.
{:.info}

### Value Function Approximation via Stochastic (Incremental) Gradient Descent

Approximate policy evaluation:

$$
\theta \in \arg \min_\theta J(\theta):= \frac{1}{2} \mathbb{E} \Big [ (Q_\theta^\pi (s_t, a_t) - y_t )^2 \Big ] 
$$

where $y_t$ is the estimate of $Q^\pi(s_t, a_t)$. 

Stochastic gradient using a single sample leads us to use

$$
g_t := (Q_\theta^\pi (s_t, a_t) - y_t) \nabla_\theta Q_\theta ^\pi (s_t, a_t)
$$

as a step direction. Note that 

$$
\mathbb{E}[g_t] = \nabla_\theta J(\theta)
$$

Check out the [Stochastic subgradient](./2021/04/24/opt-lecture-15.html) post to learn more about stochastic gradient
{:.info}

Then, we can update the parameter $\theta$

$$
\theta \leftarrow \theta + \alpha_t (Q_\theta^\pi (s_t, a_t) - y_t) \nabla_\theta Q_\theta^\pi (s_t, a_t) 
$$
Since this is a **stochastic gradient**, this is not an exact policy evaluation algorithm. This is in fact called as **inexact policy evaluation step**. Now, we need to learn how to estimate $y_t$!

## SARSA (On-policy)

### Single-step SARSA

We need to define $y_t$, which can be used as an estimation of $Q_\theta^\pi(s_t, a_t)$. One possible choice is SARSA,

$$
y_t := r_t + Q_\theta (s_{t+1}, a_{t+1})
$$

You may notice the similarity between TD(0) algorithm. However, its convergence is not theoretically clear. 

### $n$-Step SARSA

$$
y_t =r_t + \gamma r_{t+1}+ \cdots + \gamma^{n-1} r_{t+n-1} + \gamma^n Q_\theta(s_{t+n}, a_{t+n})
$$

Note that n-step SARSA uses sample data generated by the policy at each step.

$$
\pi(s) \in \arg \max_{a\in A} Q_\theta(s,a)
$$
because $y_t$ is the target for $Q^\pi$.
